Activities

College

Coding && Community

Coding && Community is a STEM-themed community service club that seeks to close the gender and race disparities in the field of computer science. I first discovered Coding && Community as a freshman during the annual Activity Fair. After attending a couple of their general meetings, I applied and accepted a volunteering position as a Java Instructor for Troy High School students. During this time, I attended their high school computer science class and directly mentored students through the programs that they were designing. Not only did this solidify my skills and confidence in Java programming, but it also helped students who were not as fortunate enough to attend a school with a solid curriculum for computer science education. I want to help these students have better access to a STEM education, and therefore, Coding && Community would like to establish an AP Computer Science A course by the end of 2023.

In addition to being a Java Instructor, I also volunteer as a STEP Instructor for Coding && Community's STEP program. The STEP program teaches computer science principles to middle and high school students in the Troy area. One of my main responsibilities is to develop the brand new curriculum for Cryptography and Cybersecurity, and taught the curriculum virtually (due to the COVID-19 pandemic).

If you would like to learn more about Coding && Community, please visit: https://codingandcommunity.org/

Association for Computing Machinery - Women's Division (ACM-W)

The ACM-W division at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute supports female and non-binary students pursuing a degree in Computer Science. Weekly meetings are held in which members can be mentored by Comptuer Science professors. ACM-W also runs a mentor program for first-year students majoring in computer science, in which we assist them in progressing through their freshman year.

If you would like to learn more about ACM-W, please visit: https://women.acm.org/

National Girls Collaborative Project (NGCP) - National Girls Advisory Board

The primary goal of the NGCP is to encourage girls to the fields of STEM. As a member of the NGCP Girls Advisory Board, I provide feedback to the projects sponsored by the NGCP. I offer support for projects through volunteering, and as a Collegiate Member, I mentor the younger women on the Advisory Board!

If you would like to learn more about the National Girls Collaborative Project, please visit: https://ngcproject.org/
